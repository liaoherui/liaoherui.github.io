About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher specializing in computational biology, with expertise in artificial intelligence, microbiology, and bioinformatics. My research focuses on developing novel computational methods to understand complex biological systems, particularly in microbiome analysis and genome annotation.

My work includes creating open-source tools like StrainScan, which provides high-resolution strain-level microbiome composition analysis, and contributing to genome annotation pipelines that accelerate biological discovery.

I am currently seeking faculty positions where I can establish an independent research program combining machine learning, computational biology, and microbiome research.

Research Overview

My research combines computational biology, machine learning, and microbiology to address fundamental questions in biological systems

Strain-Level Microbiome Analysis

Developing computational methods to resolve microbial communities at unprecedented resolution. My work on StrainScan enables researchers to identify and quantify bacterial strains in complex microbiome samples.

Key Contributions:

  • Novel k-mer based approach for strain identification
  • High-throughput analysis pipeline processing 1000s of samples
  • Validated across multiple cohorts and diseases
  • Open-source tool with 150+ GitHub stars

Machine Learning for Genomics

Applying deep learning to predict gene function, regulatory elements, and phenotypes from genomic sequences. Integrating multi-omics data for systems-level understanding.

Open Science & Tool Development

Committed to reproducible research and community-driven science. All tools are open-source with comprehensive documentation and tutorials.
